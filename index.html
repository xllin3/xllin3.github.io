About

About me

I'm a PhD candidate at the Centre for Brain, Minds and Markets, University of Melbourne, working at the intersection of behavioural economics/finance and decision science. My research examines effort and risky decision-making in complex problem-solving.

Research

Research interests

Effort allocation under complexity

How people decide how much cognitive effort to invest when facing problems of varying structural difficulty, operationalised through fitness landscape analysis of combinatorial search spaces.

Risk preference & effort aversion

Joint structural estimation of risk and effort aversion parameters using Bayesian methods, examining how the two interact in foraging-style decision tasks.

Computational & experimental methods

Designing behavioural experiments around the Knapsack Problem, with parameter recovery and model validation via Bayesian structural estimation.
